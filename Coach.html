League of Legends For noobs! 

My fellow travelers, are you perhaps interested in learning how to play League of Legends? Don't know where to start but want to play the support role? Don't fear, the Rakan main is here! Get your capes ready and be prepared to dance! I'm here to give you a newbie's perspective into how to start playing the support role in League of Legends!
**DISCLAIMER** This coaching page is for people who have ZERO experience in League of Legends but are interested in getting started, especially in the support role. I will go over the bare minimum basics on how to start playing the game, as there is so much to learn about League. As a noob myself, I hope to guide other noobs with the same mindset as me: playing the game for fun. I do not have professional tips here-- I just want to share a game that I like with other people!

What is League of Legends?
League of Legends is a 5 v 5 MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena) in which players aim to destroy the enemy team's nexus. This includes destroying objectives along the way, such as outer turrets, inhibitors, and inner turrets. There are three lanes and a jungle; within lanes, players can fight against each other and earn gold and experience by killing minions. In the jungle, junglers earn gold and experience by killing jungle camps; they often get player kills through ganking lanes.
Here are each of the roles along with descriptions:

    Top Laner: Usually a tank/brawler
  • helps secure rift herald (top side of jungle)
  • solo laner but can carry team fights
Mid Laner: Usually an assassin/mage
  • can assist other lanes by roaming
  • helps jungler secure objectives
  • solo laner but usually counters squishy enemies in team fights
Jungler:
  • secures jungle buffs and objectives
  • ganks lanes to assist with kills
Bot laner: usually an Attack Damage Carry
  • tends to be weak early game but dishes out high damage later on
  • Tends to be squishy and easy to kill
Support: usually a tank/enchanter/mage
  • supports the bot-laner
  • protects their allies and helps engage battles
  • most importantly, they protect their teammates!
